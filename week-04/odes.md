1. What a differential equation is in your own words

A differential equation is any equation containing derivatives.

2. The difference between ODE and PDE

An Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE) contains derivatives with respect to exactly one independent variable. It models simple systems that change in only one dimension, such as time.

A Partial Differential Equation (PDE) involves partial derivatives with respect to two or more independent variables. An example is the heat equation, where temperature depends on both time (t) and spatial coordinates (x,y,z).

  3. What separability means and why it matters

A separable differential equation is where all the y's are on one side and 
  all of the x's are on the other side so that you can integrate both sides  
  during the same step. 

It matters because if x and y terms are mixed together, you can't integrate both sides.
